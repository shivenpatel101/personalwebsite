For those curious about the opening animation

My favorite mathematician is Grant Sanderson, better known as 3Blue1Brown, and this intro is my tribute to the video of his that first hooked me: But what is a Fourier series? From heat flow to drawing with circles. It's my name, Shiven Patel, drawn entirely by a Fourier series. I sampled the outline of each letter into several thousand points, treated those points as a complex signal, and ran a discrete Fourier transform to break the path into rotating vectors (epicycles). Each circle spins at its own fixed frequency, and the tip of the final arm traces the curve. I keep the ~50 largest components per letter and let Manim, the Python library Sanderson himself wrote, render the spinning circles.

For the especially astute: yes, a few letters (the cursive H most of all) drift slightly as the drawing finishes. It comes down to truncating the series to a finite number of terms, sampling the path by proportion instead of true arc length (which skews the centroid, the zero frequency term), and forcing one chain of vectors to draw a letter whose mass isn't evenly spread across its strokes. The cleaner fix is to give each stroke its own set of vector arms so those awkward parts are handled independently. If you want to nerd out about that better version, reach out, or go explore Manim yourself.
